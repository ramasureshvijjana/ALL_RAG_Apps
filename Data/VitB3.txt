Short answer:
There is no difference — Niacinamide is Vitamin B3.
But “Vitamin B3” is a broad name, and niacinamide is one form of it.

Clear explanation
Vitamin B3 (umbrella term)

Vitamin B3 refers to two main forms:

Niacin (Nicotinic Acid)

Niacinamide (Nicotinamide)

Both are Vitamin B3, but they behave differently in the body and on the skin.

Niacinamide

A specific form of Vitamin B3.

Very stable and gentle.

Widely used in skincare products.

Does not cause flushing or redness.

Good for:

Controlling oil

Reducing acne

Brightening skin

Repairing skin barrier

Reducing dark spots

Niacin (another form of Vitamin B3)

Another version of Vitamin B3.

Taken mostly in supplements.

Can cause "niacin flush" (redness, warmth).

Helps with:

Cholesterol control

Energy metabolism

Final takeaway

Vitamin B3 = Niacin + Niacinamide

Niacinamide is one specific type of Vitamin B3, mainly used in skincare.
